POLICY & FAQ

Everything you need to know about ticket refunds — cancellation rules, timelines, eligibility, and contact options.

CANCELLATION RULES

Refund amounts are calculated based on when the cancellation request is submitted relative to the scheduled journey time.

Time Before Departure Ticket Type % Refunded Cancellation Fee
Train Cancelled by Railways All Classes 100% None
More than 48 Hours All Classes 90% Flat ₹30–₹60 / passenger
12 – 48 Hours AC Classes (1A, 2A, 3A) 75% 25% of fare
12 – 48 Hours Non-AC (SL, 2S) 75% 25% of fare
4 – 12 Hours All Classes 50% 50% of fare
Less than 4 Hours / After Departure All Classes 0% No refund
Waitlisted (Not Confirmed) All Classes 90% Flat cancellation fee only
ℹ️ Note: Tatkal tickets have separate cancellation rules. Tatkal tickets are generally non-refundable except in cases where the train is delayed by more than 3 hours or cancelled by Railways. Premium Tatkal tickets are fully non-refundable.
✅

Eligible for Full Refund

Train cancellations, government notifications, natural disasters, or berth non-availability after booking.

⚠️

Partial Refund Cases

Train delays exceeding 3 hours, incomplete journeys due to track disruption, or short-termination of service.

💳

Refund Payment Methods

E-tickets refunded to source (credit card, UPI, wallet). Counter tickets receive cash refund at PRS counters.

❌

Non-Refundable Situations

Tatkal/Premium Tatkal, tickets forfeited due to misuse, fraud, or tickets after scheduled departure.

HOW IT WORKS

Follow these simple steps to submit and track your train ticket refund request.

1

Gather Your Ticket Information

Before starting, keep your PNR number, journey date, ticket type, and payment details handy. The PNR is printed on your ticket or available in your IRCTC booking confirmation email.

2

Submit the Refund Request

Go to the Refund Request page and fill in the 3-step form: ticket details, reason for cancellation, and supporting documents (if needed). The form takes under 3 minutes to complete.

3

Automated PNR Verification

Once submitted, our system automatically verifies your PNR with the IRCTC database within 2 hours. You'll receive an SMS confirmation with your Reference ID (e.g., RR-XXXXXX).

4

Agent Review & Approval

A refund specialist reviews your request, verifies the cancellation policy applicable, and approves the refund amount. This usually takes 12–24 hours for standard cases.

5

Refund Initiated to Your Account

Once approved, the refund is initiated to your original payment method — UPI, bank account, credit card, or wallet. Processing typically takes 3–7 banking days depending on your bank.

6

Track Your Status Anytime

Use the Track Status page to check your refund progress at any time. Enter your Reference ID or PNR number for real-time updates. Email and SMS notifications are sent at each stage.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Answers to the most common questions about the refund process.

How long does it take to get my refund? ▼
Most refunds are processed within 48–72 hours of submission approval. Once initiated, the actual credit to your account depends on your payment method:
  • UPI / Wallets: Instant to 24 hours
  • Debit Card: 3–5 banking days
  • Credit Card: 5–7 banking days
  • Net Banking: 3–5 banking days
Can I cancel a Tatkal ticket and get a refund? ▼
Tatkal tickets are generally non-refundable. However, exceptions apply:
  • If the train is cancelled by Railways — full refund
  • If train is delayed by more than 3 hours — full refund
  • If the passenger passes away — full refund with documentation
Premium Tatkal tickets are fully non-refundable under all circumstances.
What documents do I need to submit? ▼
Document requirements vary by reason:
  • Personal cancellation: No documents needed
  • Medical emergency: Doctor certificate or hospital discharge summary
  • Train cancellation/delay: No extra documents (auto-verified)
  • Death of passenger: Death certificate
All documents should be in PDF or image format and under 5MB.
My PNR is showing as waitlisted. Can I get a refund? ▼
Yes. If your ticket is still on the waitlist at the time of chart preparation (usually 4 hours before departure), it is automatically cancelled and you receive a refund minus a small flat cancellation fee. You can submit a request through this portal to expedite the process and check status.
I booked at a counter ticket window, not online. Can I use this portal? ▼
Counter tickets (PRS tickets) can be submitted through this portal for tracking purposes, but the physical refund must typically be claimed at the original PRS ticket counter or any major railway station TDR (Ticket Deposit Receipt) counter. Our portal assists with the application and tracking, and you'll receive the required documentation to present at the counter.
The status page says 'Under Review' for more than 48 hours. What should I do? ▼
If your request has been under review for more than 48 hours, please contact our support team:
  • Use the Contact Support tab on this page
  • Provide your Reference ID (RR-XXXXXX) for priority handling
  • Call our helpline: 1800-XXX-XXXX (Mon–Sat, 9AM–7PM)
Complex cases (medical certificates, group bookings) may take up to 5 working days.
Can I request a partial refund (e.g., for one passenger in a group booking)? ▼
Yes, partial cancellations are supported. In the refund form, specify the number of passengers you are cancelling for. Each passenger's fare will be calculated separately, and the refund will reflect the applicable cancellation charges for each cancelled passenger.
What is a TDR (Ticket Deposit Receipt) and when should I file one? ▼
A TDR (Ticket Deposit Receipt) is a claim filed when you are unable to travel due to exceptional circumstances or train issues. File a TDR when:
  • Train is more than 3 hours late
  • You travelled in a lower class due to unavailability of booked class
  • You were unable to board due to unavoidable circumstances
TDR claims must be filed within 10 days of the journey date. Use this portal to initiate your TDR claim.
